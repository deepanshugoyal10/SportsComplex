WELCOME TO CRICKET

In terms of the concept of the game indoor cricket is similar to cricket. Like its outdoor cousin,
indoor cricket involves two batsmen, a bowler and a team of fielders. The bowler bowls the ball
to the batsmen who must score runs.The team with the highest score at the end of the match wins.
Despite these basic similarities, the game itself differs significantly from its traditional
counterpart in several ways, most notably on the field of play and the means by which runs are obtained.

Playing Arena

The length of an indoor cricket pitch is the same as a conventional cricket pitch, and has 3 stumps at each end,
but there the similarities end. The arena is completely enclosed by tight netting, a few metres from each side
and end of the pitch. The playing surface is normally artificial grass matting. Whilst the pitch is the same length,
however, the batsmen do not have to run the entire length. The striker's crease is in the regulation place in front of
the stumps, but the non-striker's crease is only halfway down the pitch.
